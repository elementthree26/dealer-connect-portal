Critical Recall RC-2026-EA-001

Slide-Out Mechanism Failure — Safety Recall

Affected: 2024–2026 American Eagle 45A, 45T, 45X Issued: Feb 18, 2026

Potential failure of the primary slide-out motor assembly under load conditions, posing a risk of unexpected slide-in movement while the vehicle is occupied.

REV Group has determined that a subset of 2024–2026 American Eagle coaches equipped with the Lippert Components Power Gear slide-out system may experience a binding condition in the worm gear drive assembly. This binding can cause the slide to retract without driver input when the coach is stationary. Dealers should contact all affected owners immediately to schedule inspection and motor assembly replacement at no charge. Replacement parts (Lippert P/N 368550) have been allocated to each authorized service center. Allow 2.5 service hours per slide room. Contact the REV Group Warranty team at [email protected] before beginning repairs to obtain a pre-authorization number.

Important Service Bulletin SB-2026-AR-002

Generator Coolant Line Chafing — Onan QD 12.5 kW

Affected: 2022–2025 American Revolution LE 45T, 45G Issued: Jan 30, 2026

Chafing wear detected on the generator coolant supply line routing bracket on select units, potentially leading to coolant loss and generator overheating.

Inspection of pre-delivery inventory and field reports have identified inadequate clearance between the coolant supply line and the generator compartment frame rail on affected models. Left unaddressed, the chafe wear can penetrate the coolant hose wall causing leaks and overheating shutdowns. Dealers should inspect the coolant line routing on all affected units in inventory and in service. Where chafing is found, install the protective split-loom sleeve kit (REV P/N AR-GEN-226) and re-route the hose with the updated P-clamp bracket per the attached procedure drawing. Time allowance: 1.5 hours. Submit warranty claim under campaign code SB-AR-0226.

Info Technical Notice TN-2026-ALL-001

PDI Checklist Updated to Version 3.2

Affected: All 2025–2026 Model Year Coaches Issued: Mar 1, 2026

The Pre-Delivery Inspection checklist has been updated to include additional Firefly multiplex system verification steps and revised leveling system torque specs.

Effective March 1, 2026, all pre-delivery inspections must use PDI Checklist v3.2. Key additions include: (1) Firefly System self-test with full slide and awning cycling verification, (2) Hydraulic leveling system jacking pad torque confirmation at 120 ft-lb, (3) Updated tire pressure recording format matching TPMS sensor IDs, and (4) Enhanced water system pressure-hold test extended from 15 to 30 minutes. The updated checklist is available in the Dealer Resource Center under Service > PDI Documents. All PDI completions after March 1 must reference checklist version 3.2 in the PDI record submitted with delivery paperwork.

Important Service Bulletin SB-2026-AC-004

Aqua-Hot 450D Bypass Valve Leaking at Idle

Affected: 2023–2025 American Coach Tradition, Patriot Issued: Jan 12, 2026

A batch of Aqua-Hot 450D hydronic heating units shipped with a defective mixing bypass valve that may develop a weeping leak at the valve body after extended operation.

Aqua-Hot has notified REV Group of a defective bypass valve (Aqua-Hot P/N 61-2295-D) installed in units built between March 2023 and September 2024. The valve seat material may degrade under repeated thermal cycling, resulting in a slow internal leak that reduces heating efficiency and, in severe cases, allows diesel fuel contamination of the hydronic fluid. Dealers should check the Aqua-Hot serial number against the affected range list (attached). For affected units, drain and flush the hydronic system, replace the bypass valve with the revised part (P/N 61-2295-E), and refill with Aqua-Hot approved fluid. Time allowance: 3.0 hours. Pre-authorization required — contact REV Warranty at 1-800-311-3380.

Critical Recall RC-2025-EA-001

Air Brake Actuator Line Fitting — Safety Recall

Affected: 2021–2023 American Eagle 45A, 45T Issued: Dec 5, 2025

Potential for a push-to-connect fitting on the rear air brake actuator line to disengage under sustained air system pressure cycling, resulting in partial brake loss.

NHTSA has opened investigation PE-25-042 related to this condition. REV Group has issued a voluntary safety recall in advance of NHTSA's formal recall order. On affected coaches, the rear axle air brake actuator supply line uses a push-to-connect fitting assembly (Bendix P/N K117726) that may not fully seat if contaminated with residual thread sealant during assembly. Under sustained pressure cycling the fitting can back out 1–3mm allowing air loss. Dealers must bring in all affected units for immediate inspection regardless of brake system complaint history. Replace any affected fittings with the revised crimp-ferrule assembly (Bendix P/N K117740). This service must be completed before returning the coach to customer use. Time allowance: 2.0 hours. File warranty claim under recall code RC-EA-0125.

Info Technical Notice TN-2025-ALL-003

Firefly Multiplex System Firmware v6.1.4 Released

Affected: All 2020–2026 Coaches with Firefly Gen 3 System Issued: Oct 15, 2025

Firefly has released firmware version 6.1.4 for the Gen 3 multiplex system, addressing intermittent touchscreen freeze and awning auto-retract delay issues reported by customers.

Firefly firmware v6.1.4 resolves the following field-reported issues: (1) Intermittent touchscreen display freeze lasting 10–30 seconds during simultaneous slide operation — fixed by revised CAN bus arbitration priority settings. (2) Awning auto-retract delay of up to 90 seconds after wind sensor trigger — reduced to under 5 seconds. (3) Incorrect battery state-of-charge display when using aftermarket lithium battery packs — updated SOC algorithm. The update is delivered via USB flash drive (order Firefly P/N FFW-614-USB from REV Parts). Follow the Firefly Tech Bulletin TN-FF-2025-014 for update procedure. Update takes approximately 12 minutes. No pre-authorization required — submit time as goodwill warranty.

Important Service Bulletin SB-2025-AR-002

Bedroom Slide-Out Seal Compression Adjustment

Affected: 2022–2024 American Revolution LE 45T, 45G, 44B Issued: Sep 8, 2025

Reports of water intrusion at the bedroom slide-out on select units traced to insufficient seal compression at the slide-out transition corners. Adjust per updated procedure.

Field reports indicate water intrusion at the upper transition corners of the bedroom slide-out on affected units during sustained rain events. Investigation shows the foam-bulb seal compression falls below the minimum 25% deflection spec at the slide corners when the slide room is fully extended. This is correctable by adjusting the slide travel stop to increase seal compression. Follow the updated slide travel adjustment procedure in SI AR-SLIDE-2022 Rev.B (attached). Check seal compression using the included paper-slip test at all four corners after adjustment. If seal damage is found, replace with REV P/N AR-SEAL-7802 before performing adjustment. Time allowance: 2.0 hours. Submit warranty claim under SB-AR-0925.
